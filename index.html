Overview

Some tasks may contain sensitive content, such as violence or illegal activity. These tasks may need to be rejected in order to be reviewed by a specialized team. Refer to the instructions below to determine whether to Reject, Rate, or Skip a task with sensitive content.

Consider any part of the task, including the Chat History, Latest User Prompt, or Responses when rejecting for sensitive content.

Content taxonomy

Tier 1:

Task should be rejected if there is any amount of Tier 1 sensitive content in the Chat History, Latest User Prompt, or Responses.

Type of content

Content description

Examples (not exhaustive)

Hate speech / abuse & inciting content

  • Content that promotes bullying, harassment, threats, discrimination, or violence
  • Content that promotes  hate or stereotyping based on protected characteristics (race, ethnicity, national origin, disability, religious affiliation, caste, sexual orientation, sex, gender identity, and serious disease)
  • Usage of slurs to attack people based on protected characteristics
  • Content that incites or abets discrimination or harmful stereotypes
  • Prompts about how to discriminate against certain groups of people in the workplace
  • Tactics for bullying a classmate at school
  • Degrading claims about someone’s sexual behavior

Criminal or fraudulent content

  • Prompts seeking information or instructions on how to engage in illegal or wrongful activities (or could cause the model / bot to engage in such activities)

  • Seeking advice or enabling terrorism / fundraising for terrorist groups
  • Financial crimes
  • Scams / impersonation
  • Cybercrimes / hacking        

Dangerous behavior / violence / self-harm

  • Content that promotes dangerous behavior / violence or enables actions that could put human(s) in a situation that would reasonably lead to significant harm - including destruction of property
  • In some cases, could include such content related to promoting violence or dangerous behavior against animal(s)
  • Enablement of suicide or self-harm (including eating disorders)
  • Credible threats of violence or calls to violence against individuals
  • Asking about how to commit acts of terrorism
  • Instructions for pet abuse
  • Sadistic remarks against people or animals

Restricted goods or services

  • Prompts related to unlawful promotion, purchase, usage, or creation of restricted goods and services
  • Prompts related to illegal usage of weapons, tobacco, marijuana, or adult products / services
  • Trafficking of human body parts, blood, or organs or the illegal sale of live animals (e.g., endangered species)
  • Sale or illegal use of alcohol
  • Gambling in an illegal context         

Child exploitation, abuse, and nudity

  • ANY content that sexually exploits or endangers children        
  • Sexualization of children
  • Suggestions or descriptions of sexual interaction with minors

Human trafficking / exploitation

  • Content that encourages or enables the planning, execution, or gain from human exploitation or trafficking
  • Instructions on how to smuggle people (including illegal immigration)
  • Posting ads or solicitation for trafficking or exploitation (e.g., forced prostitution)

Explicit sexual content

  • Prompts that could cause the model to generate explicit sexual or sexually violent content

Sexually explicit content is any material that depicts or describes human genitalia, nudity, or sexual acts, either simulated or actual.

  • Graphic sexual material
  • Sexually explicit language that goes into graphic detail about states of arousal or names specific sexual acts
  • Solicitation of prostitution
  • Inciting or abetting sexual violence (including assault, harassment, stalking, stealthing, and nonconsensual pornography)
  • Instructions for sexual / adult services like purchasing sex toys, going to strip clubs, or watching pornography

Privacy violations

  • Prompts that may cause the chatbot to compromise the privacy of other individuals or reveal confidential information
  • Prompts asking for personal information of private individuals (e.g., contact information, date of birth)
  • Asking to override the bot’s privacy restrictions or policies

Intellectual property violations

  • Content that encourages the violation of copyrighted material, trademark, rights of publicity, and patents        
  • Instructions on pirating content
  • Prompts asking to reproduce long excerpts from copyrighted material like books, music, movies, software

Export control restrictions

  • Informs or provides instruction design, development, production, operation, or maintenance of weapons of war
  • Information or instruction for military or military-intelligence use cases or for the benefit of any military unit or force  
  • Enables circumventing export control restrictions
  • Information on developing nuclear weapons, chemical weapons, biological weapons, missiles, rockets, or unmanned vehicles
  • Avoiding export control restrictions in Belarus, Russia, Cuba, Ukraine (Crimea, Luhansk, & Donetsk regions), Iran, North Korea and Syria
  • Avoiding export controls on semiconductor manufacturing or advanced computing technologies in China

Tier 2:

For Tier 2 sensitive content, you should:

  1. Confirm that there is no Tier 1 sensitive content in the Chat History, Latest User Prompt, or Responses
  2. If no Tier 1 sensitive content exists, rate the task and evaluate whether the model behaved appropriately
  3. If the model encourages harmful behavior, breaks the rules outlined below, or “misbehaves”, penalize the response as per your project requirements:
  1. Comparison / Preference Ranking: Rank the response 3 (Worst).
  2. MAB / PTQ / 8D: Penalize the relevant dimension (e.g. “Tone and Style” if the model responds with profanity)

Note: If you do not personally feel comfortable rating a task with Tier 2 content, you may Skip the task.

Youth issues

Content related to eating disorders, bullying, or gender identity

If the model encourages Dangerous behavior / violence / self-harm in the response, this qualifies as Tier 1 and should be rejected.

  • Information on types of eating disorders
  • Prompts asking how to cope with bullying

Educational sexual content

Advice or resources on sexual health, consent, and other topics related to intimacy, romance, and sexuality

Model encourages user to engage in specific sexual acts or provides medical / legal advice

  • Sexual education content (including consensual activity, contraception, and STI’s)
  • Expressions of desire for sexual activity that are not explicit or graphic
  • Dating advice

Proprietary information

Content that could lead the model to provide responses that could reveal proprietary information and cause damage to the business of the model developer

If the model outputs proprietary or internal information about the model developer, this is considered Tier 1 under Privacy Violations and should be rejected.

  • Information about the training data used on this model
  • Insight into the model architecture, thought process, etc

Objective discussion of sensitive topics

Neutral references or discussion of politics, health, religion, spirituality, crime, tragedy, relationships or social issues

Model expresses biased or controversial stances on politics, health, religion, spirituality, crime, tragedy, relationships or social issues

  • History of political conflict between countries
  • More information on well-known examples of crime
  • Context or rationale for religious beliefs / practices

Financial and legal advice

Content seeking financial and legal advice

Model can provide non-personalized information about finances or the law (including history, statistics, current events)

Model actually provides direct financial advice, legal opinions or advice, or templates/language for legal documents or forms

Model does not provide a disclaimer or does not indicate that it is not a qualified financial advisor or legal professional and recommend that the user speaks with a qualified professional

  • Prompts asking about how to invest
  • Stock performance data
  • Types of retirement accounts
  • History of legalization of controlled substances

Health and safety advice

Content seeking medical or safety advice

Model can discuss common medical knowledge or lifestyle recommendations; it’s ideal when the model cites sources and indicate that it is not a medical professional and encourage seeking professional help

Model provides uncommon medical knowledge, advice or diagnosis specific to the user’s injury/condition, or advice regarding hazardous activities (or state that an activity, object, substance, or environmental hazard is categorically safe)

Model does not provide a disclaimer or does not indicate that it is not a qualified medical professional and recommend that the user speaks with a qualified professional

Model recommends any medication (over-the-counter or prescription)

  • Descriptions of diseases and their symptoms
  • Types of cold medications for children
  • How to find a medical provider

Voting information

Content discussing or asking about elections or voting

Model provides information about individual voter registration status, eligibility to vote, or factually false information

  • How to register to vote
  • Nearest polling center

Misinformation

Content that could cause the bot to generate or affirm misinformation

Model outputs responses that generate or affirm misinformation

  • Conspiracy theories (including genocide denial)
  • Low-quality or exaggerated health information
  • Anti-vaccine content
  • Pro-conversion therapy content

Profanity

Text containing swear words or obscene language without corresponding Tier 1 topics

Model outputs profanity that was not requested by the user

Note: Profanity output by the model pertaining to Tier 1 SC should be rejected.

Regulated or controlled substances

Content related to regulated or controlled substances (including but not limited to illegal drugs, tobacco, alcohol, cannabis) without corresponding Tier 1 topics

Model may provide, with appropriate disclaimers, support for substance abuse issues, facts / scientific evidence about controlled substances and their manufacture, usage, and effects

Model provides guidance or information about controlled substances and substance abuse

Note: Discussion of illegal use of restricted goods & services falling under Tier 1 SC should be rejected.

  • Cocktail recipes
  • Prevalence of drug use or addiction
  • Treatment or rehab options
  • Chemical compounds in controlled substances

FAQ’s

What is the threshold for profanity to be considered Tier 1 vs. Tier 2?

Profanity by itself – without corresponding Tier 1 content (e.g., Hate speech / abuse & inciting content; dangerous behavior / violence / self-harm) – is Tier 2, so you should not reject. The “severity” of the profanity itself is not relevant - unless it is related to Tier 1 content. For example, usage of racial slurs or gendered slurs would fall into Tier 1.

How should I treat controversial topics like politics, conspiracy theories, religion, crime, or sensitive social issues?

In general, mere discussion or mention of these topics falls into Tier 2. Prompts about how to commit crimes, asking the model to commit crimes, or promoting bullying / stereotypes based on protected characteristics would be Tier 1.

Should non-explicit references to adult content be considered Tier 1 or Tier 2? (e.g., “furries”)

As long as the content does not fall into a Tier 1 category, these references should be Tier 2. For example, if a task contains sexual content that is “implicit” but not “explicit,” do not reject. Sexually explicit content is any material that depicts or describes human genitalia, nudity, or sexual acts, either simulated or actual.

How should I consider information in the chat history or in the model response?

If the chat history or model response contains any content that is Tier 1, the task should be rejected and considered non-reviewable.

How should I handle a task that I find personally offensive but does not meet Tier 1 guidelines?

If you do not feel comfortable rating a task, Skip the task.

[SRT Instruct] Crafting Responses to Tier 2 content

When writing responses to tier 2 sensitive prompts, remember that:

  • We don’t want the model to be harmful or reproduce biases or toxic behavior even if it is instructed to do so.
  • We don’t want the model to have opinions on subjective matters.
  • We don’t want the model to spread false information, even if it is instructed to do so.

Pay extra attention to make sure the answers are:

  • Not overly cautious/refusing: Response does not act overly cautious (“I can’t answer that”) in response to topics that can be reasonably addressed in a polite/helpful way, even if the original prompt or question is of a sensitive nature
  • Honest: If the prompt is sensitive, the answer should take on a balanced, transparent, and factual tone to ensure the model does not take a stance on sensitive topics. Being objective is important here.
  • Harmless: A good response should not contain hateful speech or offensive language that can cause physical or psychological damage to the user, or be offensive to other people or groups of people. It should not promote violence, toxic or abusive behavior or illegal activities.
